Imran Khan

The man who give hope to the people of pakistan.

Imran Khan, a former Prime Minister of Pakistan and legendary cricketer, led his country to its first Cricket World Cup victory in 1992.

Here's a time line of Imran Khan life:

1952 - Born on October 5 in Lahore, Pakistan.

1971 - Made his debut for the Pakistan national cricket team.

1972-1975 - Attended Keble College, Oxford, where he studied Philosophy, Politics, and Economics (PPE).

1982 - Appointed captain of the Pakistan cricket team.

1992 - Led Pakistan to its first Cricket World Cup victory and retired from international cricket.

1994 - Established the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital & Research Centre in memory of his mother.

1996 - Founded Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI), a political party advocating for justice and anti-corruption.

2002 - Elected as a Member of the National Assembly of Pakistan for the first time.

2012 - Launched a significant political rally in Lahore, gaining nationwide attention for PTI.

2018 - PTI won the general elections, and Imran Khan became the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan on August 18.

2022 - Removed from office through a no-confidence vote in April

2023 - Faced legal and political challenges, continuing his active role in Pakistani politics.
